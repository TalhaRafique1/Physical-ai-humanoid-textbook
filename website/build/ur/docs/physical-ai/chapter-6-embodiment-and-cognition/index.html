Chapter 6: Embodiment and Cognition in Physical AI

Learning Objectives​

By the end of this chapter, students will be able to:

  • Analyze the relationship between embodiment and cognition
  • Evaluate cognitive architectures for physical systems
  • Design embodied cognitive systems for real-world tasks
  • Understand the role of morphological computation in intelligence

Introduction​

The relationship between embodiment and cognition challenges traditional views of intelligence as purely computational. In Physical AI, the physical form and interaction with the environment fundamentally shape cognitive processes.

6.1 Embodied Cognition Theory​

The Extended Mind Thesis​

Embodied cognition posits that cognitive processes extend beyond the brain to include the body and environment.

Morphological Computation​

Morphological computation refers to the idea that the physical body contributes to computation, reducing the burden on central processing units.

6.2 Cognitive Architectures for Physical Systems​

Subsumption Architecture​

Subsumption architecture implements behavior through layered control structures without centralized planning.

Behavior-Based Robotics​

Behavior-based approaches decompose complex tasks into simpler, reactive behaviors.

Hybrid Deliberative-Reactive Systems​

Combining deliberative planning with reactive behaviors for flexible and robust operation.

6.3 Sensorimotor Contingencies​

Active Perception​

Active perception treats sensing as an active process involving controlled movements and attention.

Affordances​

Affordances represent possibilities for action that emerge from the relationship between agent capabilities and environmental properties.

6.4 Developmental Robotics​

Cognitive Development Models​

Developmental robotics studies how cognitive abilities emerge through interaction with the environment.

Intrinsically Motivated Learning​

Intrinsically motivated systems drive their own learning through curiosity and intrinsic rewards.

6.5 Neuromorphic and Bio-Inspired Approaches​

Spiking Neural Networks​

Spiking neural networks more closely mimic biological neural processing.

Bio-Inspired Control Systems​

Control systems inspired by biological neural circuits and sensory-motor pathways.

6.6 Consciousness and Self-Awareness​

Minimal Self Models​

Minimal self-models enable robots to distinguish between self and environment.

Predictive Processing​

Predictive processing theories suggest that the brain constantly generates predictions about sensory inputs.

6.7 Philosophical Implications​

The Hard Problem of Consciousness​

Philosophical questions about whether artificial systems can truly possess consciousness.

Moral Status of Embodied AI​

Ethical considerations regarding the treatment of sophisticated embodied AI systems.

Self-Assessment Quiz​

  1. Explain the concept of morphological computation with examples from robotics.
  2. Compare subsumption architecture with traditional planning-based approaches.
  3. Describe how sensorimotor contingencies contribute to perception in embodied systems.
